01 — Problem Statement

Operations teams lose 40+ minutes
per incident to fragmented tools

Analysts, managers, and engineers juggle dashboards, ticket tools, and chat — critical context lost at every handoff. AI flags are dismissed because no one explains the "why."

⏱

15–40 minutes to form a hypothesis

Users spend 60% of investigation time manually verifying across 3+ tools rather than acting on the anomaly.

🔀

3–5 tool context-switches per incident

Dashboard → Excel → Jira → Slack → wait. Every handoff leaks context. Teams re-solve the same problems repeatedly with no shared decision log.

🤖

AI flags exist — but no one trusts them

No confidence scores, no cited sources, no "why." Users dismiss AI alerts and verify manually, completely negating any efficiency gains from automation.

📊

20+ metrics, zero visual hierarchy

Dense dashboards take 2–5 minutes just to find the first critical signal. "Investigate now" looks identical to "informational" — causing missed anomalies.

Design Challenge
How might we design a single AI-powered interface that takes operations teams from alert → investigation → action → report → share in under 10 minutes, without ever leaving the dashboard?

05 — Design Decisions

Before & after — issues found,
evidence gathered, redesigned

Issues surfaced by Claude + Figma MCP heuristic evaluation and confirmed in live usability sessions. Each became a Stitch AI redesign prompt.

Before — H-09 · P0 Critical · Broken Dropdown

"e igh" — text layer overflow rendering bug

The ticket priority dropdown was completely broken — displaying "e igh" due to text overflow. The most critical field in ticket creation was unusable. Found by Claude MCP evaluating node 277:1089.

After — Pill Button Group Selector
Priority
Crit
High
Med
Low

4 pills: Critical / High / Medium / Low

Selected state filled purple, others outlined. Pre-populated from alert severity detected during investigation. Instant visual confirmation — no ambiguity about what was selected.

Before — H-12 · P1 High · Report Flow
GENERATE & SEND REPORT
CANCEL

Single button — immediate send, no review

Clicking "Generate & Send" dispatched the report instantly. PRD required a mandatory preview. Violated heuristic H5 (Error Prevention) — AI errors could be sent directly to stakeholders.

After — Generate → Preview → Send

3-step enforced flow with mandatory preview

Helper text added: "You'll review before it's sent." The Generate Report screen (277:1756) confirmed this redesign. All 5 usability test participants rated the preview step 5/5 satisfaction.

Before — U-03 · Testing Found · Root Cause Hidden
Investigation Confidence
82%
↓ jumps to interventions
root cause? 2 screens away

Causality analysis buried — 3 of 5 users never found it

Investigation jumped from confidence score directly to suggested interventions. P3 (Manny) rated root cause clarity 1/5 and said the flow "jumped from step 2 to step 5." The lowest satisfaction score: 3.80/5 for AI trust.

After — Root Cause as Mandatory Step 3

Step 3 in guided flow — impossible to skip

Causality analysis promoted to Step 3 with bold "ROOT CAUSE" header and left accent border. Mandatory in the flow. Expected to raise AI trust score from 3.80/5 → 4.3+ in the next test cycle.

09 — AI Tools Throughout

6 AI tools · every phase ·
~20 hours of workflow accelerated

End-to-end experiment in AI-assisted UX. Each tool served a specific, deliberate role — AI accelerated without replacing critical design judgment.

Research Phase
Looppanel
Auto-coded 5 interview transcripts into themes, pain points, and user needs. Generated persona inputs and affinity cluster summaries from observational research.
Cut 8+ hrs of qualitative coding to ~45 min. Surfaced "context-switching" as the #1 dominant theme across all 5 users.
Lo-Fi + Hi-Fi Prototype
Stitch AI
Screen-by-screen generation for all 23 hi-fi screens using a Master Prompt design system + per-screen detail prompts. 8 redesign iteration cycles.
Vague prompts → generic screens. Specific prompts naming exact screen IDs and component states → production-quality UI. Prompting is a design skill.
Heuristic Evaluation
Claude + Figma MCP
Reviewed all 23 screens via get_screenshot + get_design_context on each node. Evaluated against Nielsen's 10 heuristics with PRD attached for full context.
Found cross-screen inconsistencies impossible to spot manually. The "oira→Jira" label bug (H-14) and contradictory report flows (H-12) required full-file context.
Usability Testing
Otter.ai
Auto-transcription of 5 Zoom sessions (40–65 min each). AI chat used to extract confusion moments, success quotes, and issues by task number.
Surfaced Manny's root cause complaint and Abhishek's tick mark ambiguity within minutes of session end. Both became high-severity redesign items.
Analysis + Data
ChatGPT
Structured real-time metric entry during sessions. Usability score calculation. Gap analysis comparing test results against PRD UX requirements.
Flagged P4 Task 4 as 4.5× statistical outlier (13.5 min vs 3-min target) during analysis — immediately directing focus to the sharing confirmation gap.
Data Visualization
Julius AI
4 publication-quality charts from usability data: task time comparison, satisfaction by category, error distribution, usability score breakdown donut.
Generated charts matching exact brand colors and report formatting. Reduced chart production from ~2 hrs to 15 min per chart set.

11 — Key Takeaways

What designing an AI product
with AI tools taught me

1

AI evaluation + self-assessment are complementary

AI caught visual and structural issues in minutes. Self-assessment caught interaction consequences — the accidental dismiss, the missing confirmation state. Neither alone was sufficient. The combination was the method.

2

Transparency is the prerequisite for AI trust

The lowest score (3.80/5) was "Trusted AI confidence score." Users weren't distrusting AI — they wanted to understand it. Showing the formula and cited sources mattered more than the number itself.

3

One outlier tells the most important story

P4's 13.5-min Task 4 looked like statistical noise. But the Otter.ai transcript revealed the most critical design gap: zero post-sharing feedback. Qual + quant together — never either alone.

4

The PRD is your north star — keep returning

Both "Not Met" requirements were in the PRD from day one. They only surfaced under real usability test conditions — not expert heuristic evaluation. Real users navigating under task pressure exposed what expert review missed.

5

Precise prompts produce precise outputs

Vague Stitch AI prompts produced generic screens. Prompts that named specific Figma screen IDs, listed exact component states, and referenced PRD requirements produced production-quality UI. Prompting is a design discipline.

6

Every research hour compounds forward

The 51 user needs from research shaped 55 PRD requirements, which shaped 23 screens, which were tested and validated. The quality of the final product was determined in the first two weeks of user research.
